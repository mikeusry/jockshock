## The story we're selling

Don't pitch a product. Pitch one of three angles, depending on outlet.

| Angle | Best for | Hook |
|---|---|---|
| **A. The founder story** | UGA media, Athens local, Georgia podcasts, alumni mags | Four UGA D1 athletes — including a Honda Award gymnast and a viral "world's strongest cheerleader" — launch a gear-care brand together |
| **B. The gym-channel pivot** | Trade press (SGB Media, Athletic Business), franchise media | Why a D1 Training franchise owner is launching JockShock + ZeroPoint as a paired subscription program for facilities |
| **C. The category problem** | Sports parents publications, athlete service publications, lifestyle | "The gear bag in your trunk is the dirtiest thing you own" — a category-level take on equipment funk, with us as the authority |

The Gear Smell Field Guide PDF is the universal hook — it gives reporters something to link to and an excuse to cover us without doing a product review.

## Goal: 3 placements before we light up the strip

We don't run a logo strip until we have **three real, linked placements**. Order of attack — easiest first:

1. **D1 Training Athens newsletter / blog** *(Fred, this week)*
2. **One Athens-area podcast** *(UGA-adjacent — gymnastics, football, or general sports)*
3. **One trade publication** *(SGB Media, Athletic Business, or Franchise Insights — gym-channel angle)*

Stretch goal: a *Sports Business Journal* or *Inside Edition*-style hit (Anna's network — she's been on GMA + Inside Edition before).

## What you give the journalist

For every pitch, attach:

1. **One-pager** — what JockShock is, the 4 founders + their UGA athletic credentials, the gym-channel program with Fred. *(I can build this from the brand brief if you don't have a one-pager yet — just ask.)*

3. **Three founder photos** — Mike + Karin, Fred + Anna, all four together if you have one
4. **One product photo on a clean background** — the matte-black bottle, hero pack
5. **One photo of a JockShock-stocked gym** — once D1 Training Athens has shelves, this becomes the money shot

## Hard rules

- **No fake placements.** No "AS SEEN IN" strip until we have 3 real ones. ([feedback_no_fake_press_strip](../../.claude/projects/-Users-mikeusry-CODING-JockShock/memory/feedback_no_fake_press_strip.md))
- **Every logo links to its article.** FTC + reader trust.
- **No "EPA-registered" / "kills bacteria" / "24-hour" anywhere in pitches.** Those phrases get us banned from trade publications and rejected by serious reporters. ZeroPoint Technology is the trust language.
- **Disclose Anna + Fred + Karin's relationships when they pitch** their networks (FTC §255 — material connection to the brand). They can pitch — they just identify themselves as co-founders.
- **No paid press releases.** PR Newswire / Business Wire wires don't count as placements and tank trust.

## When we have placements

Replace the credentials strip with a real "AS FEATURED IN" logo strip:
- Greyscale logos at ~60% opacity
- Eyebrow: "AS FEATURED IN"
- Each logo wraps a link to the actual article (FTC requirement)
- Order: largest publication first → smallest. Don't lead with D1 Training Athens unless it's the only one.
