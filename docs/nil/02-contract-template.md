## NAME, IMAGE, AND LIKENESS ENDORSEMENT AGREEMENT

This Name, Image, and Likeness Endorsement Agreement (the **"Agreement"**) is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] (the **"Effective Date"**) between:

**Southland Organics, Inc.**, a Georgia corporation with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS], Athens, Georgia (**"Southland"** or the **"Company"**), and

**[ATHLETE NAME]**, an individual student-athlete enrolled at [SCHOOL] competing in [SPORT] (the **"Athlete"**).

Southland and Athlete are each a **"Party"** and together the **"Parties."**

---

### 1. Engagement and Brand

1.1 Southland engages Athlete to provide endorsement, content, and activation services in support of **JockShock**, a Southland Organics brand offering athletic gear deodorizer products (the **"Brand"** and the **"Products"**).

1.2 Athlete agrees to provide the services described in Section 2 (the **"Services"**) and to grant the rights described in Section 6 (the **"Rights"**) in exchange for the compensation described in Section 5 (the **"Compensation"**).

1.3 The Parties understand that this Agreement is intended to comply with the NCAA name, image, and likeness ("NIL") rules in effect as of the Effective Date, the State of Georgia's NIL law, and the policies of [SCHOOL] (collectively, the **"NIL Rules"**).

---

### 2. Services and Deliverables

2.1 During the Term, Athlete will produce and publish content promoting the Brand on Athlete's own social media accounts according to the following monthly cadence:

  (a) **Two (2) Instagram posts per month**, of which one (1) shall be a Reel and one (1) may be a still image or carousel
  (b) **One (1) TikTok post per month**
  (c) **One (1) long-form video per month** (Instagram Reel, TikTok, or YouTube Short, Athlete's choice) telling a personal-routine story (e.g., gear-care routine, training prep, season recap) in which the Brand is featured organically
  (d) **One (1) in-person activation per month** at a Southland-coordinated event (e.g., photo shoot, gym tour, program visit) located in Athens, Georgia or within reasonable driving distance, for which Southland will provide reasonable travel reimbursement

2.2 In addition to the monthly cadence, Athlete agrees to one (1) **launch content drop** in the first calendar month of the Term introducing the partnership.

2.3 Athlete shall use her reasonable best efforts to publish content evenly across the Term rather than batching deliverables.

2.4 Athlete may decline a specific creative direction or in-person activation that conflicts with her training schedule, academic obligations, or personal values. Southland will work in good faith to reschedule or substitute.

---

### 3. Term and Termination

3.1 **Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for **five (5) months** (the **"Term"**), unless terminated earlier under Section 3.

3.2 **Renewal.** The Parties may extend the Term by mutual written agreement at any time before its expiration.

3.3 **Termination Without Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement without cause on thirty (30) days' written notice. In such case, Southland will pay Athlete a pro-rated portion of the monthly base payment for any partial month worked, and any earned per-order bonus through the termination date.

3.4 **Termination for Material Breach.** Either Party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within ten (10) business days of receiving written notice.

3.5 **Termination for NIL Compliance.** If [SCHOOL]'s NIL compliance office, Southland's compliance counsel, or a competent regulatory authority determines that this Agreement is not in compliance with the NIL Rules, either Party may terminate immediately on written notice. Southland will pay Athlete a pro-rated portion of compensation through the termination date.

3.6 **Termination by Southland for Conduct.** Southland may terminate immediately on written notice if Athlete:

  (a) is charged with a serious crime (felony, or any misdemeanor involving violence, dishonesty, or substance abuse);
  (b) engages in hate speech, harassment, threats, or discriminatory conduct on any public platform;
  (c) publicly endorses or promotes the use of illegal drugs, performance-enhancing drugs, or substances banned by the NCAA;
  (d) engages in conduct that would, in Southland's reasonable judgment, bring substantial public disrepute on Southland, JockShock, or the partnership.

  In the event of termination under this Section 3.6, Southland's payment obligations cease as of the termination date and any unearned pro-rated base payment is forfeited. Earned per-order bonuses through the termination date remain payable.

---

### 4. Content Standards and Approval

4.1 **Content Approval.** Before publishing any content where Southland, JockShock, or the Products are tagged, mentioned by name, or visually featured, Athlete will submit the content to Southland for review. Southland will respond within forty-eight (48) hours, providing approval, requested edits, or rejection. Failure to respond within forty-eight (48) hours constitutes approval.

4.2 **Approval Standard.** Southland will approve content unless it (a) makes a factual claim about the Products that requires substantiation Southland does not have, (b) fails to comply with FTC advertising disclosure rules (see Section 4.3), (c) appears to violate NIL Rules, or (d) raises a material brand-safety concern.

4.3 **FTC Disclosure.** Athlete will include clear and conspicuous disclosure of the paid relationship in every published Brand-related post, in accordance with the U.S. Federal Trade Commission's Endorsement Guides. Acceptable disclosures include `#ad`, `#sponsored`, or `Paid partnership with @jockshock`. Disclosures will appear at the start of captions and within the first three lines of long captions.

4.4 **Removal.** Athlete agrees to remove or edit content within twenty-four (24) hours of Southland's reasonable written request if the content violates this Section 4.

4.5 **Off-Brand Content.** Athlete remains free to publish her own non-Brand content as she sees fit. This Agreement does not restrict Athlete's general social media use, personal opinions, or non-competing endorsements (subject to Section 7).

---

### 5. Compensation

5.1 **Base Compensation.** Southland will pay Athlete **$8,000.00** in base compensation, paid in five (5) equal monthly installments of **$1,600.00**, each due on the first business day of each month of the Term, paid by ACH transfer to Athlete's designated account.

5.2 **Performance Bonus.** Southland will pay Athlete **$4.00 per First-Time Order** (defined below) placed using Athlete's Discount Code (defined in Section 6.2) or originating from Athlete's Vanity URL (defined in Section 6.3), capped at **$4,000.00** total over the Term.

  (a) A **"First-Time Order"** is an order placed by a customer who has no prior order on Southland's records and who completes the purchase within sixty (60) days of clicking Athlete's Vanity URL or applying Athlete's Discount Code.
  (b) Performance bonuses are calculated monthly and paid by the fifteenth (15th) day of the month following the calendar month in which orders were placed.
  (c) Southland will provide Athlete with a monthly attribution report showing first-time orders, total orders, and bonus calculation.

5.3 **Payment Mechanics.** All payments are subject to applicable tax withholding and reporting. Athlete will provide a completed IRS Form W-9 before the first payment. Southland will issue an IRS Form 1099-NEC by January 31 of the following calendar year.

5.4 **Expenses.** Southland will reimburse Athlete for reasonable, pre-approved travel expenses associated with in-person activations under Section 2.1(d). Athlete will submit receipts within thirty (30) days. Southland will reimburse within fifteen (15) business days of receipt.

---

### 6. License Grant and Attribution Rights

6.1 **NIL License.** Athlete grants Southland a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license during the Term to use Athlete's name, image, likeness, voice, and approved content (collectively, the **"NIL Materials"**) in:

  (a) Southland's organic social media channels
  (b) Southland's owned digital properties (websites, email, SMS)
  (c) Paid advertising on Meta, TikTok, Google, YouTube, and similar platforms
  (d) Press, public relations, and earned media

6.2 **Discount Code.** Southland will create a discount code in the form **`[ATHLETE-CODE]`** (e.g., `ELENA20`) that grants 20% off the customer's first order. The code is a non-expiring code attributed to Athlete for the duration of the Term and ninety (90) days thereafter.

6.3 **Vanity URL.** Southland will create and own a vanity URL in the form **`southlandorganics.com/go/[athlete-slug]`** that redirects to the JockShock product page. The slug is reserved for Athlete during the Term and ninety (90) days thereafter.

6.4 **Post-Term License.** After the Term, Southland's license under Section 6.1 continues for an additional twelve (12) months on a non-exclusive basis, limited to organic social media reposts and case-study or "wall-of-partners" usage. Paid advertising rights end at Term end unless extended in writing.

6.5 **Attribution Reporting.** Southland will share aggregated campaign performance with Athlete monthly. Athlete may publicly cite aggregated outcomes (e.g., "drove $X in first-time orders") with Southland's prior approval. Athlete will not disclose Southland's internal CAC, margin, or LTV figures without permission.

---

### 7. Exclusivity

7.1 During the Term, Athlete agrees not to enter into a paid endorsement or sponsorship arrangement with any **Competing Product**, defined as: athletic gear deodorizer sprays, athletic gear cleaning sprays, or athletic equipment-cleaning products marketed primarily to athletes or athletic programs (including, without limitation, Clear Gear, Vapor Fresh, 2Toms, Defunkify Sport).

7.2 **Carve-outs.** Section 7.1 does not restrict Athlete from:

  (a) wearing or using competing products her school issues to her or her team
  (b) accepting sponsorship from non-competing brands (apparel, footwear, food/beverage, financial services, etc.)
  (c) personal-care products that are not marketed for gear cleaning (e.g., body deodorant, laundry detergent, fragrances)

7.3 **Post-Term Restriction.** For thirty (30) days after the Term ends, Athlete will not enter into a paid endorsement with a Competing Product. After thirty (30) days, no restriction applies.

### 8. School Compliance

8.1 Athlete will register and disclose this Agreement with [SCHOOL]'s NIL compliance office (e.g., NIL Go or equivalent system) before publishing any content under it.

8.2 Athlete will provide Southland with confirmation of compliance registration within ten (10) business days of the Effective Date.

8.3 Both Parties will cooperate in good faith with any reasonable request from [SCHOOL]'s NIL compliance office, the NCAA, or a state regulator regarding this Agreement.

### 9. Representations and Warranties

9.1 **Athlete represents and warrants that:**

  (a) She has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement
  (b) Performance of this Agreement will not breach any other agreement to which she is a party
  (c) She is not currently subject to a suspension, declared ineligibility, or pending NCAA investigation that would prevent her from performing under this Agreement
  (d) Her published content under this Agreement will be original and will not infringe any third party's intellectual property rights
  (e) She will keep accurate records of any income received under this Agreement for tax purposes

9.2 **Southland represents and warrants that:**

  (a) It has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement
  (b) The Products are EPA-registered through ZeroPoint subregistration as marketed
  (c) It will not require Athlete to make claims about the Products that Southland cannot substantiate
  (d) It will pay Athlete on the schedule in Section 5

### 10. Indemnification

10.1 Each Party will indemnify the other against third-party claims arising from its own breach of this Agreement, gross negligence, or willful misconduct.

10.2 Neither Party will be liable to the other for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.

10.3 Southland's total liability under this Agreement is capped at the greater of (a) total Compensation paid to date or (b) Compensation owed for the remainder of the Term.

---

### 11. Confidentiality

11.1 Each Party will keep the other Party's non-public business information confidential during and for two (2) years after the Term.

11.2 Confidential information includes Southland's CAC, LTV, margin, supplier, and formulation details, as well as Athlete's payment terms and personal information.

11.3 Either Party may disclose the existence of this Agreement and its general nature (i.e., "I am a paid partner of JockShock"). Specific financial terms are confidential.

### 12. General

12.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Georgia, without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

12.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising under this Agreement will first be addressed in good-faith discussion between the Parties. If unresolved after thirty (30) days, the Parties agree to non-binding mediation in Athens, Georgia. If mediation fails, the Parties may seek redress in the state or federal courts located in Clarke County, Georgia.

12.3 **Independent Contractor.** Athlete is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner of Southland. Nothing in this Agreement creates a joint venture, partnership, or employment relationship.

12.4 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties on the subject. It supersedes any prior discussion, proposal, or understanding.

12.5 **Amendments.** Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.6 **Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force.

12.7 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except Southland may assign to an affiliate or successor in connection with a corporate transaction on written notice to Athlete.

12.8 **Notices.** Notices under this Agreement will be sent by email and confirmed by U.S. mail to the addresses below.

### Notes for the lawyer

- This template assumes Georgia governing law and a UGA-affiliated athlete. If an athlete from a different state or school is being onboarded, governing-law and compliance-system references must be updated.
- The 90-day post-term carry on discount code and vanity URL (Sections 6.2 and 6.3) is intentional — it captures any orders that happen after Term end but were driven by the Athlete's content during the Term. Worth keeping unless counsel sees a problem.
- The 30-day post-term competing-product restriction in Section 7.3 is short by industry standard (60-90 days is more common). Kept short here as a goodwill / friendship-of-the-deal gesture. Counsel can extend if needed.
- Section 5.2 caps performance bonus at $4,000. Confirm with Mike whether to raise to $6,000 or remove cap before sending.
- Indemnification cap in Section 10.3 is intentionally tied to compensation. Some athletes' representatives will push back; we can negotiate.
